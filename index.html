About Me

I am a visiting assistant professor of physics in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Amherst College.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Kentucky, where I worked in the groups of Renee Fatemi and Tim Gorringe.

I completed my Ph.D. in Physics at Boston University in May 2023, where I worked on the Muon g-2 Experiment at Fermilab and was advised by Rob Carey.

I am interested in using precision measurements to test models of nature at its most fundamental level. I work on the Muon g-2 Experiment, as well as the PIONEER experiment, a next generation rare pion decay experiment that will take place at the Paul Scherrer Institute (PSI) in Switzerland. You can find my publications on Inspire Hep and Google Scholar.

Previous Experiences

I received my BA in Physics (with a minor in philosophy) from Colgate University in 2016.
